# Gilt-Edged Models (GEM)

## Agent-Model Accounting

The accounting at the heart of all my agent-based (computational) monetary system models is based principally, but not only, on the sectoral accounting systems presented by Wynne Godley and Marc Lavoie (G\&L) in their book [Monetary Economics](/reading). Incrementally sophisticated agent-based models (ABMs) include model *simple* (ABMSIM), *portfolio choice* (ABMPC) and *liquidity preference* (ABMLP). ABMLP is a system in which perpetuities (consols) are never redeemed. In a simple behavioural relationship between the government and household sector, whatever bonds are demanded by households are supplied by the government.

The most recent model, agent-based model liquidity preference \- extended (ABMLP-X), is a representation of a system that extends the earlier models with the inclusion of a public debt management office (DMO), a commercial (private) bank and a financial asset fund manager. Each class agent has its own brain (logic), its own behaviours (goals) and its own balance sheet.

The DMO is mandated to issue a sophisticated array of long and short term debt instruments; the private bank not only creates loans and deposit monies for households and firms, but acts as the government’s primary dealer with functional auto-collateralisation, liquidity support and collateral repurchase arrangements with the central bank.

#### Architecture & Presentation

Models are written in the [Python](https://www.python.org/) programming language using the project [Mesa](https://mesa.readthedocs.io/stable/) framework. View a brief sketched [overview](https://www.data-reports.net/giltedged-systems/sketch/gilt-edged-overview.html) of the project.
